Fuel Consumption Hwy (L / 100 km)

City and highway fuel consumption ratings are shown in litres per 100 kilometres (L / 100 km)
The following formula is used to calculate fuel consumption in litres / 100km.
(Litres used X 100) ÷ km travelled = Litres per 100km.
Example a car uses 65 litres to travel 500km
(65 X 100) ÷ 500 = 13

Fuel Consumption Comb (mpg)

The combined rating (55% city, 45% hwy) is shown in L / 100 km and in miles per gallon (mpg).
This can be calculated like the following example:
City MPG: 30 (55 / 100 × 30 = 16)
Highway MPG: 40 (45 / 100 × 40 = 18)
Combined MPG: 16 + 18 = 34
